Our Mission

Connecting Crime Victims with Help, Advocacy & Education

C.H.A.N.C.E. is a North Carolina nonprofit dedicated to connecting crime victims with the help, advocacy, navigation, counsel, and education they need to understand and exercise their rights within both legal and social systems.

Our Initial Project: Military-connected survivors of sexual assault, harassment, and domestic violence — a population that falls through the gaps between military victim counsel services and civilian legal resources. C.H.A.N.C.E. is building the foundation for a recognized competency standard in this area, supporting survivors from the moment they seek help through final resolution.

C.H.A.N.C.E. provides education, navigation, and limited legal representation in select cases. We are a new and developing organization, not a comprehensive victim services provider.

Our Story

A New Organization Built to Close Critical Gaps

Founded by Diva Edel

C.H.A.N.C.E. was originally established by Diva Edel, who recognized that many crime victims in North Carolina lacked clear information about the rights guaranteed to them under Marsy's Law — the constitutional amendment passed by North Carolina voters in 2018.

Diva created C.H.A.N.C.E. to provide education, community awareness, and support for individuals navigating the criminal justice process. Her founding work established the organization's core values: advocacy, clarity, and the belief that every victim deserves to be informed.

Leadership Transition to Ruth A. Cresenzo, J.D.

As the organization developed, Ruth A. Cresenzo, J.D. assumed leadership and launched C.H.A.N.C.E.'s first focused project: serving military-connected victims of sexual assault, harassment, and domestic violence — an area where she identified critical unmet need during her decades of military legal service.

Drawing on decades of experience in military justice and Special Victims' Counsel programs, Ruth saw that survivors navigating both military and civilian systems had almost nowhere to turn for coordinated support — and built C.H.A.N.C.E. to fill that space while honoring the organization's founding commitment to victims' rights.

Initial Project

Serving Military-Connected Victims & Families

Our Current Focus: C.H.A.N.C.E.'s inaugural project serves military-connected victims of sexual assault, harassment, and domestic violence. We provide education, system navigation, and connection to legal resources. Limited legal representation is offered on a selective, capacity-based basis and is not available in all cases. When representation is not available, we focus on helping individuals understand their options and connect with appropriate resources.

If you are a service member, Guard or Reserve member, veteran, military spouse, intimate partner, or family member affected by crime, you may be navigating:

  • A military investigation (CID / NCIS / OSI)
  • Command actions, MPOs, or UCMJ proceedings
  • Civilian criminal charges, DV court, or protective orders
  • Family court or custody proceedings
  • VA claims, appeals, or MEB / PEB processes
  • Multiple systems running at the same time

C.H.A.N.C.E. helps you see the whole picture and understand how these processes fit together, so you can make informed decisions.

Safety First

If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
For confidential sexual assault support, contact the DoD Safe Helpline: 877-995-5247.

Gaps in Military Victim Counsel (SVC / VLC)

Special Victim Counsel provide critical services, but their authority can be limited by branch regulations, geographic lines, civilian court restrictions, and caseload. We help identify where your SVC can help and where additional civilian support is needed.

Connecting You to Legal Resources

We help you prioritize your legal needs, identify the right type of attorney, and provide warm referrals to civilian attorneys experienced in military-connected domestic violence, sexual assault, custody, and VA matters.

Services

For Professionals & Organizations

Professionals supporting military-connected survivors often feel unprepared when cases cross military and civilian systems. C.H.A.N.C.E. partners with attorneys, advocates, DV and SA agencies, courts, and organizations to improve outcomes for this population.

Professional education and technical assistance offered by C.H.A.N.C.E. are guided by Ruth A. Cresenzo, J.D., whose background in military justice and victim advocacy informs the organization's approach.

Who We Partner With

Civilian attorneys and law firms  ·  Military and civilian victim advocates  ·  DV and SA agencies  ·  Legal aid organizations  ·  Prosecutors' offices  ·  Courts and clerk offices  ·  Colleges and universities  ·  Multidisciplinary teams  ·  Military family support programs

Technical Assistance & Consultation

We provide case-specific consultation to attorneys and organizations, including strategy discussions on military vs. civilian jurisdiction, mapping collateral consequences of legal options, and helping civilian counsel understand military culture, documents, and processes.

To request technical assistance, email ruth@ncchance.org with your contact information and a brief description of your request.

Training & Education

We offer trainings, workshops, and presentations tailored to your organization's needs. Topics include:

  • Military Victim Counsel 101 — what SVC / VLC can and cannot do
  • MPOs vs. DVPOs and cross-system protective orders
  • Coordinating cases with simultaneous military and civilian proceedings
  • Military sexual assault and domestic violence response systems
  • Trauma-informed practice for military-connected survivors
  • VA, MEB/PEB intersections with criminal and family matters
  • Safety planning in dual-military or mixed-status situations

Waitlist for Trainings

Professional Development

Training & Education

C.H.A.N.C.E. is committed to building a recognized practice area for professionals who serve military-connected victims of sexual assault, harassment, and domestic violence. We train attorneys, advocates, and organizations to competently navigate the intersection of military and civilian systems.

Training is educational in nature. It does not replace professional licensure or legal training and is not certification-based at this stage. We are intentionally building capacity step-by-step as the organization grows.

Training for Attorneys

We train civilian, legal aid, and private attorneys on representing military-connected clients — including SVC/VLC collaboration, MPO/DVPO enforcement, CID/NCIS/OSI navigation, cross-state custody, VA/MEB/PEB intersections, and trauma-informed representation.

Training for DV/SA Organizations

Customized training for domestic violence and sexual assault agencies on military culture, dual-system safety planning, and how to collaborate with military victim counsel and civilian attorneys when both systems are involved.

Training for Prosecutors

Education for prosecutors' offices on the intersection of civilian criminal cases and military justice, including coordination with command, SVC/VLC, and CID/NCIS/OSI, and managing parallel proceedings.

Training for Courts & MDTs

Programs for judicial officers, court staff, and multidisciplinary teams on military justice processes, military documentation, and the unique jurisdictional dynamics affecting military-connected survivors.

Training for Advocates

For victim advocates working in military and civilian settings — how to identify gaps, when to involve civilian counsel, and how to help survivors navigate both systems simultaneously.

Future: Competency Framework

C.H.A.N.C.E. is laying the foundation for a structured competency framework for attorneys representing military-connected victims — including training modules, a resource library, mentorship, and certificates of completion.

Leadership

Meet Our Executive Director

Ruth A. Cresenzo, J.D.

Executive Director, C.H.A.N.C.E.  |  Retired Lieutenant Colonel, U.S. Army

Judge Advocate Victims' Rights Attorney Special Victims' Counsel Title IX Coordinator & Investigator Nonprofit Management – Duke University Adv. Victim Comprehensive Specialist Mediator Educator & Trainer

Ruth A. Cresenzo, J.D., is a retired Lieutenant Colonel, attorney, and nationally recognized advocate for military-connected crime victims. She began her legal career as a general practitioner in Greensboro, NC (1996–2004), handling family, immigration, juvenile, and criminal law — including international child abduction cases and asylum petitions, for which she received an Award of Merit from the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children. She was also recognized with a Certificate in Non-Profit Management from Duke University (2004) and went on to chair community boards and co-found a community nonprofit, establishing a foundation in civic leadership that has informed her work ever since.

Ruth's military career spans over 25 years of National Guard service, including two deployments — Operation Enduring Freedom (Kuwait, 2006–2007) and the Multinational Force & Observers Mission (Egypt, 2012–2013), where she served as Command Judge Advocate advising 13 international contingents. She became one of the inaugural Special Victims' Counsels in the National Guard SVC Program in 2014, representing over 80 servicemembers and veterans in an emerging area of military law. Her leadership roles grew to include:

  • Senior Regional SVC – South Region — managed 6 attorneys and 2 paralegals across 12 states and territories, 100+ clients
  • Deputy Program Manager, National Guard Bureau SVC Program — led a team of 40+ Judge Advocates and paralegals serving 500+ clients; co-chaired the working group that responded to the Fort Hood/Vanessa Guillén Independent Review Committee recommendations
  • Special Legal Advisor, Virgin Islands National Guard — advised the Adjutant General on sexual assault, military justice, and federal/state policy

Additionally certified as a Title IX Coordinator and Investigator, a Special Victims' Investigator, an Advanced Victim Comprehensive Specialist (National Advocate Credentialing Program, 2023), and an Army Mediator (2024), Ruth brings one of the most comprehensive credential sets in the field to her leadership of C.H.A.N.C.E.

Nonprofit & Volunteer Leadership

Ruth's nonprofit and community service work is woven throughout her career. She holds a Certificate in Non-Profit Management from Duke University, has served as chair of community boards, and co-founded a community nonprofit. As a volunteer Victim Advocate at Camp Virginia during her Kuwait deployment, she also played a founding role in establishing a USO presence at Camp Virginia. Her legal assistance program for North Carolina National Guard soldiers — which she developed from the ground up — earned the ABA LAMP Distinguished Service Award (2010) and the Army Chief of Legal Assistance Award (multiple years, most recently 2023). She has also served as a DSS Facilitator for Domestic Abuse, Neglect and Dependency cases in civilian courts, and has conducted nationwide outreach and training in collaboration with community legal organizations, DV/SA agencies, and state coalitions.

As Executive Director of C.H.A.N.C.E., Ruth leads the organization's nonprofit operations, strategic development, statewide partnerships, and training programs — carrying forward a lifetime of community service into an organization built to last.

Our Founder

Diva Edel

D

Founder, C.H.A.N.C.E.

C.H.A.N.C.E. was founded by Diva Edel, a victims' rights attorney who saw firsthand how many crime victims in North Carolina were unaware of the rights guaranteed to them under Marsy's Law — the constitutional amendment passed by North Carolina voters in November 2018.

Diva founded C.H.A.N.C.E. in 2023 after identifying a gap in access to legal services related to Marsy's Law and crime victims' rights. She built the organization on the belief that empowerment begins with understanding — and that every victim deserves access to accurate, clear information about the system designed to protect them.

Her founding vision established the organization's core values of advocacy, education, and victim-centered support that continue to guide C.H.A.N.C.E. today.
