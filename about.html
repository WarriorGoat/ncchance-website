Our Story

A New Organization Built to Close Critical Gaps

C.H.A.N.C.E. was founded by Diva Edel, a victims' rights attorney who identified a gap in access to legal services for crime victims in North Carolina. After voters passed Marsy's Law in 2018, Diva recognized that many victims still did not understand the protections available to them. She founded C.H.A.N.C.E. in 2023 to provide education, awareness, and legal advocacy so that no victim would navigate the justice system alone.

When Ruth A. Cresenzo, J.D. — a retired Lieutenant Colonel, Judge Advocate, and nationally recognized authority on military-connected victim advocacy — assumed leadership of the organization, she continued the commitment to all crime victims while launching the organization's first primary initiative focused on military-connected survivors.

Drawing from more than 25 years of military legal service, including senior leadership in developing and resourcing the National Guard's Special Victims' Counsel program, Ruth recognized that military-connected victims routinely fall into gaps between systems. Under her direction, C.H.A.N.C.E. is developing a volunteer attorney network model, expanded professional training, and building toward competency standards in this critical practice area.

C.H.A.N.C.E. today stands on a foundation of victims' rights and education for all crime victims, with a focused first initiative building expertise, partnerships, and a model designed to expand across all areas of victim representation.

Meet Our Executive Director

Ruth A. Cresenzo, J.D.

Executive Director, C.H.A.N.C.E.  |  Retired Lieutenant Colonel, U.S. Army

Judge Advocate Victims' Rights Attorney Special Victims' Counsel Title IX Coordinator & Investigator Nonprofit Management – Duke University Adv. Victim Comprehensive Specialist Army Mediator Educator & Trainer

Ruth A. Cresenzo, J.D., is a retired Lieutenant Colonel, attorney, and nationally recognized advocate for military-connected crime victims. She began her legal career as a general practitioner in Greensboro, NC (1996–2004), handling family, immigration, juvenile, and criminal law — including international child abduction cases and asylum petitions, for which she received an Award of Merit from the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children. She was also recognized with a Certificate in Non-Profit Management from Duke University (2004) and went on to chair community boards and co-found a community nonprofit.

Ruth's military career spans over 25 years of National Guard service, including two deployments — Operation Enduring Freedom (Kuwait, 2006–2007) and the Multinational Force & Observers Mission (Egypt, 2012–2013), where she served as Command Judge Advocate advising 13 international contingents. She became one of the inaugural Special Victims' Counsels in the National Guard SVC Program in 2014, representing over 80 servicemembers and veterans in an emerging area of military law.

Her leadership roles included:

  • Senior Regional SVC – South Region — managed 6 attorneys and 2 paralegals across 12 states and territories, 100+ clients
  • Deputy Program Manager, National Guard Bureau SVC Program — led a team of 40+ Judge Advocates and paralegals serving 500+ clients; co-chaired the working group responding to the Fort Hood/Vanessa Guillén Independent Review Committee recommendations
  • Special Legal Advisor, Virgin Islands National Guard — advised the Adjutant General on sexual assault, military justice, and federal/state policy

Additionally certified as a Title IX Coordinator and Investigator, a Special Victims' Investigator, an Advanced Victim Comprehensive Specialist (National Advocate Credentialing Program, 2023), and an Army Mediator (2024), Ruth brings one of the most comprehensive credential sets in the field to her leadership of C.H.A.N.C.E.

Nonprofit & Volunteer Leadership

Ruth holds a Certificate in Non-Profit Management from Duke University, has served as chair of community boards, and co-founded a community nonprofit. As a volunteer Victim Advocate at Camp Virginia during her Kuwait deployment, she also played a founding role in establishing a USO presence at Camp Virginia.

Her legal assistance program for North Carolina National Guard soldiers earned the ABA LAMP Distinguished Service Award (2010) and the Army Chief of Legal Assistance Award (multiple years, most recently 2023). She has also served as a DSS Facilitator for Domestic Abuse, Neglect and Dependency cases, and has conducted nationwide outreach and training in collaboration with community legal organizations, DV/SA agencies, and state coalitions.

Our Founder

Diva Edel

D

Founder, C.H.A.N.C.E.

C.H.A.N.C.E. was founded by Diva Edel, a victims' rights attorney who saw firsthand how many crime victims in North Carolina were unaware of the rights guaranteed to them under Marsy's Law — the constitutional amendment passed by North Carolina voters in November 2018.

Diva founded C.H.A.N.C.E. in 2023 after identifying a gap in access to legal services related to Marsy's Law and crime victims' rights. She built the organization on the belief that empowerment begins with understanding — and that every victim deserves access to accurate, clear information about the system designed to protect them.

Her founding vision established the organization's core values of advocacy, education, and victim-centered support that continue to guide C.H.A.N.C.E. today.

Advocacy and education for crime victims in North Carolina. Initial project: military-connected survivors.
